ABSTRACT

Tourism plays a pivotal role in the advancement and promotion of a nation, contributing significantly to its visibility and appeal. However, countries are currently confronted with the challenge of effectively promoting their tourism offerings to both domestic and international audiences. To bridge this gap, many nations are leveraging user-friendly technological tools to enhance awareness of their tourism destinations.
Recognising the immense potential of India's tourism sector and the absence of a comprehensive platform facilitating connections between tourists and destinations, we have embarked on a project to address this need. Our platform will prioritise user-friendly navigation, enabling seamless exploration of various destinations across India. It will provide detailed information on popular tourist spots, encompassing historical landmarks, natural wonders, cultural attractions, and lesser-known locales.
Furthermore, our platform will offer customisation options such as trip duration, hotel preferences, and budget constraints, empowering users to craft personalised itineraries effortlessly through an intuitive drag-and-drop interface. Additionally, we will incorporate a forum feature to foster connections among like-minded travellers, facilitating the exchange of experiences and insights on various tourism-related topics.
Through our initiative, we aim to stimulate economic growth in India's tourism sector while also promoting greater socio-economic equity across different regions of the country.

2.1.1 TripAdvisor’s Trip Builder
TripAdvisor's Trip Builder is an innovative online tool designed to simplify the travel planning process. With a user-friendly interface and a wealth of resources, Trip Builder allows travellers to customise their itineraries, ensuring a personalised experience tailored to their interests and preferences. Whether you're a solo adventurer, a family on vacation, or a couple looking for a romantic getaway, Trip Builder provides the tools needed to create unforgettable travel experiences.
Key Features
Customisable Itineraries:
Easily plan your trip by adding attractions, restaurants, and activities that match your interests and schedule.
Interactive Map:
Visualise your travel plans with an integrated map that helps you organise your itinerary based on location, making it easier to navigate your destination.
Recommendations and Reviews:
Access a vast database of user-generated reviews and ratings to make informed decisions about where to go and what to do.
Collaborative Planning:
Share your trip plans with friends and family, allowing for collaborative input and feedback on the itinerary.
Budgeting Tools:
Estimate costs for your trip by viewing pricing information for accommodations, activities, and dining options, helping you stay within your budget.
Advantages 
Enhanced Personalisation through AI Recommendations:
Trip Builder’s AI-driven algorithms analyse user preferences, past behaviours, and similar traveller profiles to suggest personalised recommendations. This allows users to see curated options based on interests, budget, and travel style, such as solo or family travel, enhancing the relevance of suggestions and creating a tailored travel plan.
Dynamic Adaptability for Real-Time Customisation:
Leveraging AI, Trip Builder offers real-time itinerary adjustments based on changing preferences or circumstances, such as weather changes or unexpected closures. Users can adjust their plans dynamically, and the platform will generate alternate suggestions or time allocations, ensuring a smooth and flexible travel experience even on short notice.
Intelligent Recommendations Based on Location and Interests:
With AI, Trip Builder can provide location-based suggestions that align with user interests, such as nearby dining spots, hidden attractions, or unique cultural experiences that other users with similar tastes have enjoyed. This feature helps travellers uncover hidden gems tailored specifically to their interests, which can enhance the overall travel experience.
Time-Efficient Trip Planning through Streamlined, AI-Filtered Options:
AI-powered sorting and filtering save users time by showing them the most relevant options upfront. Rather than sorting through an overwhelming amount of content, users receive targeted suggestions based on their preferences, making the planning process faster and more efficient.
Consistent Refinement of Recommendations via Machine Learning:
Trip Builder learns from each user’s engagement with the platform, continuously refining its recommendations over time. For frequent travellers, the platform becomes increasingly accurate, presenting a progressively better-suited travel experience each time it’s used. This iterative improvement supports loyalty and helps the platform become an increasingly reliable travel companion.
Disadvantages
Potential for Algorithmic Bias in Recommendations:
While AI can improve personalisation, it can also create biases in recommendations based on past preferences or highly rated/popular spots, potentially limiting users' exposure to new experiences. This bias may cause the platform to over-prioritise familiar or mainstream destinations rather than suggesting unique or off-the-beaten-path locations.
Privacy Concerns with Data Usage and Personalisation:
To deliver personalised experiences, the AI algorithms analyse personal data, such as past bookings, browsing behaviour, and demographic information. While this enables personalisation, it also raises privacy concerns, as users may feel uneasy about the extent of data collection needed to provide tailored recommendations.
Dependence on Data Quality for AI Effectiveness:
The AI’s ability to deliver accurate recommendations depends heavily on the quality and breadth of available data. In cases where there is limited information on newer or lesser-known destinations, recommendations may be less accurate or overly reliant on popular spots, which could diminish the user experience for those seeking unique travel options.
Risk of Narrowing Choices Due to Personalisation:
Personalised suggestions may limit the variety of options shown, inadvertently filtering out activities or destinations that could have been a good fit outside of previous patterns. This “filter bubble” effect might prevent users from exploring novel experiences that differ from their past interests, leading to a more homogenised travel experience.
Over-Reliance on AI for Travel Decision-Making:
The AI-driven interface may reduce users’ autonomy by heavily influencing their choices based on pre-set algorithms. While this can simplify planning, it might also lead users to depend on the platform's recommendations over personal research, potentially missing out on richer experiences that require a more proactive, hands-on approach to discovery.

2.1.3 Booking.com: The biggest hotel booking service in the world
Booking.com: Agency Model vs. Merchant Model
Booking.com primarily uses the agency model, which differs from the merchant model used by some other online travel agencies (OTAs) like Expedia:
Agency Model: In this model, Booking.com serves as an intermediary, connecting customers with hotels. Hotels control the room rates and availability while Booking.com earns a commission on each booking. Payment is generally made directly to the hotel upon check-in or check-out, giving the hotel control over pricing and customer service. This approach also allows travellers the option to pay later and typically includes more flexible cancellation options.
Merchant Model: In contrast, the merchant model involves the OTA purchasing rooms in bulk at a discounted rate, then reselling them to customers with a markup. Payment is made upfront to the OTA, rather than the hotel, which can offer competitive pricing but often involves less flexibility in cancellation or refund options.
Booking.com’s choice of the agency model prioritises flexibility and direct customer relationships, particularly beneficial for travellers seeking adaptable bookings.
Key Features
Wide Inventory: 
Booking.com has a vast range of accommodations worldwide, including hotels, vacation rentals, apartments, and unique options like treehouses.
Flexible Cancellation Policies: 
Many listings offer options for free cancellation, appealing to travellers needing flexibility.
User Reviews and Ratings: 
Verified reviews help customers make informed choices based on real guest experiences.
Genius Loyalty Program: 
Frequent users benefit from exclusive discounts, room upgrades, and perks like complimentary breakfast.
Price Match Guarantee: 
Booking.com matches lower prices found elsewhere to ensure competitive pricing.
Mobile App Convenience: 
The app allows easy booking management, personalised recommendations, and quick access to reservation details.
Advantages of Booking.com Using an Agency Model
Low Operational Risk for Booking.com: 
Booking.com does not need to purchase inventory upfront. They act as a mediator, reducing risks associated with unsold inventory.
Flexibility for Partners (Hotels/Hosts): 
Hotels and accommodation providers retain control over pricing, availability, and policies, making the model more appealing to businesses.

Scalable Business Model:
Booking.com can easily onboard more partners without needing to invest in inventory, enabling rapid expansion into new markets.
Simplified Revenue Stream:
The commission-based revenue is straightforward and predictable, as Booking.com earns a percentage from each successful booking.
Improved Customer Trust:
Customers pay the accommodation provider directly, which can enhance trust and confidence in their transactions.
Disadvantages of Booking.com Using an Agency Model
Dependency on Partners:
Booking.com depends on its partners to provide quality service, availability, and pricing, which can impact the customer experience if not managed well.
Limited Control Over Pricing:
Since partners set their own prices, Booking.com has limited influence on pricing strategies, which could make it harder to remain competitive.
Delayed Revenue Collection:
Booking.com earns its commission only after the customer completes their stay, delaying revenue realisation compared to the upfront payments in a merchant model.
Lower Margins: 
Compared to the merchant model, where platforms can mark up prices, the agency model has smaller profit margins due to reliance on a fixed commission structure.
Risk of Cancellation:
If customers cancel bookings, Booking.com loses its commission revenue, which could affect earnings.
Increased Competition:
The same model is used by many competitors, leading to intense competition in attracting both customers and partners.

2.1.5 Relevant Research 
Title: Smart Destinations and the Evolution of ICTs: A New Scenario for Destination Management?
Authors: Josep A. Ivars-Baidal, Marco A. Celdrán-Bernabeu, Jose-Norberto Mazón, Ángel F. Perles-Ivars
Publication: Current Issues in Tourism, October 2017
Abstract
This paper examines the transformative impact of information and communication technologies (ICTs) on tourism destination management, proposing a smart tourism destination (STD) model that integrates strategic, instrumental, and applied technological levels. Using the Delphi technique, the authors gather expert insights into the STD approach's feasibility, advantages, and limitations. The findings suggest that, while emerging ICTs such as big data and IoT enhance data-driven decision-making and visitor experience, successful implementation hinges more on effective governance and stakeholder cooperation than technology alone. The study highlights the short-term importance of these technologies, projecting significant influence on destination management within 2–5 years.
Key Observations
The Role of ICT in Tourism Management: 
ICTs provide real-time data capabilities that transform the tourism experience, allowing destinations to better meet visitor expectations through personalized services. This data-driven approach aligns with the trend towards experience-driven tourism and supports sustainability efforts by enabling more efficient resource management.
Governance as a Core Challenge: 
The success of STDs depends largely on robust governance frameworks that facilitate cooperation among stakeholders. Technology alone is insufficient; collaborative, strategic governance is essential to address barriers like interoperability, high costs, and limited public-private collaboration.


Emerging Technology Impact: 
Big data analytics, IoT, and mobile connectivity are identified as high-impact technologies, expected to transform tourism within a short term (2–5 years). Technologies such as augmented reality and autonomous vehicles are anticipated to mature later but are forecasted to play significant roles in enhancing the tourist experience and operational efficiencies.
Advantages
Enhanced Tourist Experiences:
The STD model enables destinations to deliver personalised, real-time tourist interactions, enhancing visitor satisfaction and creating memorable experiences. This allows destinations to stand out by tailoring experiences to individual preferences, leveraging big data and analytics.
Competitive Edge and Sustainability:
STDs improve the operational efficiency of destinations, offering an advantage over traditional models. By integrating ICTs, destinations can optimise resource management, especially in ecologically sensitive areas, and use real-time data to respond dynamically to visitor needs.
Increased Collaboration and Innovation:
The model fosters collaboration among tourism operators, technology providers, and local governments, driving innovation and supporting new product development that aligns with evolving tourist expectations.
Disadvantages
High Implementation Costs:
Implementing ICT-driven destination management systems requires significant upfront investment, which can be challenging for smaller destinations with limited budgets. Continuous updates and maintenance add to the financial burden, potentially limiting accessibility to this model.
Privacy and Security Concerns:
As STDs depend heavily on data collection, they raise privacy and cybersecurity issues. Safeguarding visitor data is critical to maintaining trust, yet costly cybersecurity measures and compliance with data protection regulations are additional challenges for destination managers.
Technological Dependency and Skills Gap:
The reliance on advanced technology can reduce human interaction, which may be a preference for some tourists. Additionally, the rapid pace of technological change demands continuous staff training, which can be challenging, particularly in tourism sectors with high turnover rates and limited ICT expertise.

Notifications and Updates Module: As the trip approaches, users receive real-time notifications about flight changes, weather updates, or even recommendations for local events happening during their stay. This module ensures that users stay informed about any last-minute changes and can adjust their itinerary accordingly. Push notifications are sent to the user’s phone, ensuring they don’t miss important updates.
Review and Feedback Module: Once the trip is complete, the app prompts the user to leave feedback. This module asks for ratings on accommodations, activities, and travel experiences. Users can also write reviews and share their thoughts, which helps the app improve future recommendations. This feedback loop ensures that the app continually evolves based on real user experiences.

DISCUSSION

4.1 Discussion on the proposed activity
Our tourist app is set to redefine the way people travel by creating a fair, engaging, and sustainable platform for discovering local destinations. At its core, the app empowers users to highlight hidden gems through a democratic, user-voted system. This ensures that even the most underrated attractions receive the attention they deserve, based purely on merit and user feedback, rather than commercial promotions. In doing so, the app celebrates authenticity, encouraging travellers to go beyond typical tourist hotspots and immerse themselves in local culture.
Tourism, in its traditional sense, often overlooks smaller, less-commercialised destinations in favour of heavily advertised spots. This app changes that by levelling the playing field. Lesser-known attractions and businesses—such as family-owned restaurants, independent tour guides, and artisans—gain visibility, ensuring that tourists have access to unique and enriching experiences. At the same time, these local businesses benefit from increased footfall, leading to economic growth and the creation of new job opportunities. By connecting travellers directly with local service providers, the app creates a symbiotic relationship that benefits both parties.
The fairness of the app is driven by its meritocratic rating system, where reviews and recommendations are based on real user experiences. Unlike other platforms that often promote businesses based on paid ads, this system ensures transparency and authenticity. When users vote for destinations or leave reviews, their input helps shape the app’s ecosystem, directing attention to places and businesses that genuinely excel. This not only builds trust among users but also incentivises businesses to maintain high standards, as their success is directly tied to customer satisfaction.
Beyond its focus on fairness and visibility, the app addresses a growing need for sustainability in tourism. Overcrowding in popular destinations often leads to environmental degradation and loss of cultural integrity. The app encourages travellers to distribute their visits more evenly, reducing pressure on overburdened locations. It also actively promotes responsible travel practices, such as using eco-friendly options, minimising waste, and supporting businesses that prioritise sustainability. By featuring “green” businesses and destinations, the app fosters a culture of environmental responsibility.
One of the standout features of the app is its focus on fostering community engagement. It goes beyond being a directory of destinations; it creates a platform where locals and travellers can interact meaningfully. Local residents can share insights about their communities, recommend lesser-known attractions, and offer cultural tips.
